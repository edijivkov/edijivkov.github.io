3D Models

Vietnam War Helmet

2015

A 3D model of a helmet based on the M1 helmet - a combat helmet that was used by the United States military from World War II until 1985. The helmet has added personal touches of it's wearer – little metal trinkets, a card, a flip lighter and a couple of 7.62 Rifle Rounds. The camo pattern used is the infamous Mitchell pattern, which was used heavily during The Vietnam War era.
Tools: Model - 3Ds Max.

Saw

2015

A vintage Sadvik hand saw with a rusty and run-down look. The rusty effect is achieved using a Normal Map of the rust texture and the Roughness Map of the metal texture. The Wood wear effects on the handle were created using a Normal Map of the black marble texture.
Tools: Model - 3Ds Max.

Lego Pilot

2017

The model is an authentic reproduction of the Lego Series 3 Old Timer Pilot Mini-figure. The base body and head were created with the help of the official Lego Mini-Figure patented technical plans with accurate dimensions. Web Design

Pako Commerce

2016

A concept website made for my father’s company. The site features a Menu with three buttons: About us – Information about the company, Services – Services that the company provides, Premises – What and where the shops and establishments are on the building grounds. There is also a contact button, which leads to the contact page and a website visit counter on the bottom left. The site’s background is an edited version of a free black polygon vector background by Vectorss. The logo was designed using raster shapes in Adobe Photoshop.
Tools: WYSIWYG editor, Adobe Photoshop.

Techsmart

2016

A concept website for a specialized hypermarket that sells electronic appliances and all things technology. The website uses elements from real electric appliance stores namely Technopolis. The site has two menus: Top Row Menu – Here you can learn more about the company itself, where the stores are located, news and events, all of the different promotions currently running, job opportunities, leasing policies and contact information, should you need it. The other Menu is the Main Menu – Here you can see all of the products that the company is selling, split up into 9 different categories (Computers, Periphery, Telephones and Tablets, Television sets, Photo and Video, Electrical Appliances, Audio, Fun, Auto) which are then divided into subcategories. The front page is occupied with promotions – the top section is for big promotions and deals and the bottom one is for minor ones. Both are dynamic and have an auto-scroll, which shows all of the current sales.
The Logo was created using Adobe Illustrator and the images used were touched up using Adobe Photoshop.
Tools: WYSIWYG editor, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator.
